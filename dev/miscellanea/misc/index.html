Miscellanea

In the observer-to-emitter scheme the initial momenta are past-directed and pointing inwards. This is valid because of the following reason. The Lioville vector field on the tangent bundle is the vector field which generates the geodesic flow. The component of this field along the fibers is invariant to sign inversion on the fiber. This means that the geodesic passing through the point $(x^μ,k^μ)$ and the one passing through $(x^μ,-k^μ)$ project to the same curve on the base spacetime. We do it this way so the geodesic integrator kernel is common to both transport schemes. Otherwise, although the physical equations would be the same, the numerical integrators would be different because we would have to take backward steps in the observer-to-emitter case. Once we have the solutions, we invert the sign of $k^μ$ when we use it elsewhere. ispositionat_source should't be used in condition/affect. Its purpose is to classify the endstate of a geodesic already integrated, not to condition the geodesic integration.

Coordinates are assumed to be have either cartesian or spherical topology, and to be ordered like t, r, θ, φ or t, x, y, z. The first coordinate is assumed to be timelike, and the other three spatial.
